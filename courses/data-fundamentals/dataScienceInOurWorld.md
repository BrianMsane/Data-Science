# Data Science in Our World

Many businesses are becoming more and more data-driven. This means that they conduct data science project to uncover the business insights which are burried in data. This is made possible by the field of data science. Data science is the understanding of the world through the scientific analysis of digital data. It combines the scientific method, statistics and math, specialized programming, advanced analytics, artificial intelligence, and even story telling to uncover and explain the business insights which are buried in the data. The primary goal is to extract value from the data.

The science needed in data science projects includes linear algebra, statistics, and probability. Techologies include Big Data, predictive analytics, business intelligence, data mining, and machine learning. As discussed, data comes in structured or unstructured form. These link up in data science projects.

Being curious is one characteristic of data scientist. Normally and typically, they you "Why?" to unfold the insights. There is a methodology called the "5 Why" which basically states that in order to find to root cause of a particular effect, you need to ask "Why" five times. This is used when you are modelling a cause-and-effect situation which is what data scientists find themselves working with almost all the time.

First ask "Why" and get the answer for it, the answer leads you to the next "Why" to ask and this iterates five times. It is important to note that you might not reach the cause after the first five questions so you need to repeat the question and that happens in a case whereby you have multiple roots or causes for an effect.


## Data Science Methodologies

A methodology is a general strategy that guides activities within a process. It provides a framework that can guide data scientist how to proceed with whatever methods and processes they will use to obtain answers and results. The three classical methodologies used are:

- CRISP-DM - cross-industry standard process for data mining
- SEMMA - sample, explore, modify, model, assess
- KDD - Knowledge discovery on database

They use data mining methods, best suited for strucutured data, useful for descriptive and predictive analytics, and all share common activities like data gathering, transformation, modelling, and model evaluation. They are useless on projects that work with unstructured data.
